Hi! My name is Kimmy! I am a passionate game developer and software engineer who has worked on various professional and personal projects from 2015 until now! Listed are a few of my favorite personal / semipersonal projects throughout the years.

Wyvern

A hurricane preparedness simulator utilizing IBM technology

×

Wyvern is a 3d, crossplatform natural disaster simulator that teaches users the steps they need to take in their own home to be prepared for natural disasters. I programmed object interactions and was in charge of level design. This game was created in 24 hours at HACKRPI for the CodeResponse challenge.

Bet The Farm

A strategic, open world farming simulator made in Unity3d

×

Bet The Farm was a 3d, interactive simulation of the "real life" BEST robotics course. This allowed our drivers to test different game strategies, speeds, and parts before creating and testing them in real life. After this use, the simulation was converted into a game where the player can corral pigs, pick corn, sort and harvest lettuce and tomatoes, and plant seeds all within three minutes, just as the actual robotics course. After play testing at the Bowling Green State University science fair, I decided to get rid of the three minute limit and seed planting. This was the first time one of my games created a line nearly out the door to play. At the competition, there was a similar reaction to the point where my friends had to make a time limit per player. In the end, our team won first! Unfortunately, the last version of the game was presented on my old laptop and was not uploaded to Unity's cloud service, so I only have images of previous builds. I was largely inspired by MySims Kingdom for the art style and, little did I know, pig corralling portions of the game. This game shaped my childhood and as my first non personal Unity game, I hope I did it justice. I learned how to take feedback as a developer, code simple AI, and gained a better understanding of what makes a game addicting. Some challenges I faced were definitely switching from PC to Xbox controls as the switch made picking up objects with a "click" a bit more difficult to figure out as a then beginner game programmer.

Fruits of your Labor

An open world game teaching kids the power of their community garden!

×

Fruits of your Labor was an open world hackathon project for the DandyHacks Hackathon. Our project aimed to show children the magic of community gardens and how they can help their communities. In the game, you play as a child and complete multiple tasks such as planting, growing, and harvesting tomatoes for rewards such as a salsa party or a new swing set for the school. I worked on programming while my partner did the art. The main challenge for me was not overscoping the project. I conquered this challenge by working on the game in steps and adding features as other big steps were accomplished.
